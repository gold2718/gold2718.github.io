Injera

Ingredients

IngredientAmount
Teff flour1/2 cup
Water3/4 cup
Salt1/8 tsp, optional
Oil or cooking sprayDepends on pan

Procedure

  1. Combine teff flour and water in bowl. If using starter, add half of the starter to the mixture. If you are making a larger batch, you might need a longer fermentation time to get a good dough. Be sure to refresh your starter.
  2. Mix in a dash of salt (if desired).
  3. Let sit for several hours covered with breathable material.
  4. When the mixture is proofed (plenty of bubbles forming), bring 1/4 cup water to boil in a small saucepan. Stir in 1/8 cup of the injera batter, whisking until it thickens.
  5. Stir the heated mixture back into the batter and also add water until the batter has the desired consistency (something close to crepe batter). The final mix should be able to be easily poured into the pan.
  6. Heat a skillet to medium. Some people use non-stick pans, I use both a stainless steel skillet with a light coating of olive oil spray and a well-seasoned cast-iron skillet with a light coating of olive oil.
  7. Pour batter into the skillet in a spiral pattern starting from the outside and working towards the center. This pouring technique seems to work better than trying to tilt the pan to spread the batter.
  8. The batter should form bubbles that pop. Once they pop, cover the pan and take it off the heat for a 2-3 minutes. Be careful to not let any condensation drip into the pan which can make the injera gummy and/or soggy. Remove and serve warm.
  9. Repeat with more batter taking care to recoat the pan if necessary.

Starter (levain)

To speed up the fermentation of new batches of injera, you can maintain a Teff flour sourdough starter. Follow these directions.

  1. Combine 1 cup teff flour with 1.5 cups water in a bowl.
  2. Let sit for 2-5 days until bubbles form. Check once or twice daily and if any dark liquid forms, pour it off before remixing.
  3. When mixture has bubbles and starts getting a nice, sour smell, place in jar and refrigerate.
  4. Maintain starter by pouring off any dark liquid that forms and refreshing every few days (at least once a week).
  5. To refresh starter, remove part of starter (use for making batch of injera) and replace with fresh ingredients (1.5 parts water to 1 part teff). Alternatively, remove add all the starter, mix with fresh ingredients (same ratio), and replace original amount after mixing.
